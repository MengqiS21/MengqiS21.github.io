Publications

2026

When Humans Don't Feel Like an Option: Contextual Factors That Shape When Older Adults Turn to Conversational AI for Emotional Support

Mengqi Shi, Tianqi Song, Zicheng Zhu, Yi-Chieh Lee. CHI EA 2026.

TL;DR: Older adults sometimes prefer AI over friends/family for emotional support — not out of general preference, but in specific moments: when loved ones are unavailable, to avoid feeling like a burden, or to protect their dignity. Interviews with 18 older adults reveal how concerns around independence and self-image drive these situational choices, with implications for designing more context-aware AI companions.

  • Qualitative Methods
  • Responsible AI
  • Thematic Analysis
  • Human-AI Interaction

2026

Designing with Tensions: Older Adults' Emotional Support-Seeking Under System-Level Constraints in Conversational AI

Mengqi Shi, Tianqi Song, Zicheng Zhu, Yi-Chieh Lee. DIS 2026. Under Review

TL;DR: Interviews with 18 older adults show that they often turn to conversational AI for emotional support when human support feels inaccessible, but safety interventions such as redirection or interruption can feel abrupt, undermine emotional pacing, and reduce users' sense of agency. The work argues for emotionally supportive AI systems whose safety mechanisms better align with older adults' social contexts and lived experiences.

  • Qualitative Methods
  • Conversational AI
  • Responsible AI
  • Older Adults

2026

Learn AI with AI: Designing Conversational Agents to Support Older Adults in Learning about AI

Eugene Tang KangJie, Tianqi Song, Mengqi Shi, Yi-Chieh Lee. IJHCS 2026. Under Review

TL;DR: This work examines how conversational agents can support older adults in learning about AI. Across interviews and a comparative study, the findings suggest that peer-like agents can better foster engagement and support confidence, pointing to design opportunities for AI literacy tools tailored to older adults.

  • AI Literacy
  • Older Adults
  • Conversational Agents
  • Learning Design

Taking Stock of Concept Inventories in Computing Education: A Systematic Literature Review

Murtaza Ali, Sourojit Ghosh, Prerna Rao, Raveena Dhegaskar, Sophia Jawort, Alix Medler, Mengqi Shi, Sayamindu Dasgupta. International Computing Education Research Conference (ICER) 2023.

TL;DR: This work synthesizes the concept inventory literature in computing education through a review of 65 papers and large-scale dataset analysis, surfacing major trends, gaps, and methodological patterns in how conceptual understanding is assessed.
